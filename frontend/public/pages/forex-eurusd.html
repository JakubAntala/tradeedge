EUR/USD

The pair you learn forex on. The most traded instrument on the planet, with the tightest spread, the cleanest technicals, and the most behaved liquidity sweeps. If you can't make money on EUR/USD, you won't make it on anything else.

~11 min read Major Pairs Start here
TL;DR - the pair in one paragraph

EUR/USD is the world's most liquid pair - roughly 24% of all forex volume. That liquidity gives it the tightest spread (0.0-0.3 pip raw), the cleanest reactions to levels, and the most predictable session profile. It moves on the EU-US monetary policy divergence (ECB vs Fed), inversely tracks DXY, and explodes in the London-NY overlap (13:00-17:00 NY). Master this pair first. Everything else is a variation.

Why EUR/USD is the starting pair

If forex were a music school, EUR/USD would be the piano - the instrument you learn the fundamentals on before touching anything else. There's a reason every textbook, every YouTube tutorial, every prop firm demo starts with it. Three reasons actually.

One - it's the most liquid. 24% of the entire $7.5 trillion daily forex volume flows through this single pair. That liquidity means tight spreads, instant execution, and price action that respects technical levels because there are enough participants to actually defend those levels.

Two - the fundamentals are clean. You have exactly two players to track - the ECB and the Fed. Compare that to GBP/USD where you also need to track Brexit aftershocks and UK political risk, or USD/JPY where the BoJ does weird things nobody else does.

Three - it's boring in the best way. EUR/USD doesn't spike 200 pips on a random Tuesday morning. It moves in measurable, repeatable ranges. That predictability is exactly what a new trader needs to actually see their edge work over a sample of trades.

EUR/USD is the boring pair you marry. GBP/USD and USD/JPY are the exciting pairs you date once you can afford the breakup.

Who's pulling each side - the fundamentals

Every forex pair is a tug-of-war between two central banks. For EUR/USD, the ropes are:

  • The European Central Bank (ECB) - sets the deposit facility rate, runs Asset Purchase Programmes, gives the Thursday after each meeting press conference where Lagarde basically decides EUR direction for the next 6 weeks
  • The US Federal Reserve (Fed) - sets the federal funds rate, runs QT/QE cycles, releases the FOMC statement every 6-8 weeks plus a dot plot quarterly

The driver is rate differential. When the Fed is hiking faster than the ECB, dollars become more attractive to hold (you earn more yield), capital flows into USD, EUR/USD falls. When the ECB plays catch-up and starts hiking, the differential narrows, EUR strengthens, EUR/USD rises.

That's the whole macro story in two sentences. The complication is timing - markets price in expected moves 6-12 months in advance, so by the time the ECB actually hikes, the move is often already done.

The DXY relationship - your secret indicator

EUR/USD is roughly 57% of the DXY (Dollar Index) basket. That means EUR/USD and DXY are mathematically near-perfectly inversely correlated. If DXY goes up, EUR/USD goes down. Almost always. To three decimal places.

DXY components (and their weights): EUR 57.6 % JPY 13.6 % GBP 11.9 % CAD 9.1 % SEK 4.2 % CHF 3.6 %

Practical use: when you're about to long EUR/USD, pull up DXY on a separate chart. If DXY just broke a key support, EUR/USD will likely break the mirror-image resistance. If DXY is consolidating below resistance, expect EUR/USD to also stall.

I personally have DXY in my workspace next to EUR/USD permanently. Half my entries are confirmed by DXY doing the inverse move at the same level. The pair gives you two charts for the price of one.

Real example - using DXY for confirmation

EUR/USD approaches 1.0850 resistance after a clean uptrend.

DXY is sitting on 103.50 support at the same time.

If DXY bounces hard off 103.50 with a clean reversal candle, EUR/USD almost certainly rejects 1.0850. Short setup confirmed by two charts.

If DXY breaks 103.50 cleanly, EUR/USD breaks 1.0850 and continues. Don't fight the dollar.

Volatility profile - how much it actually moves

Average True Range (ATR) on EUR/USD daily is roughly 60-90 pips depending on the macro regime. Quiet ranges (low volatility periods) can be 40 pips. Active news weeks can be 120-150 pips.

That makes EUR/USD a medium-volatility pair. Wider than USD/CHF and EUR/CHF (40-60 pips daily), tighter than GBP/USD (80-130 pips daily), much tighter than GBP/JPY (130-200 pips daily).

Session behaviour - where EUR/USD actually lives

Each session has a personality. Learn them and you'll know which kind of setup to even bother looking for.

Asian session (20:00 - 02:00 NY)

EUR/USD is on lunch break. Volume from European desks is zero, US is asleep, and Asian flows go mostly into USD/JPY, AUD/USD, NZD/USD. You'll see EUR/USD drift sideways in a 15-25 pip range. Trade plan: don't. Mark the Asian range high and low - those become liquidity targets for London open.

London open (02:00 - 05:00 NY)

European desks wake up, EUR liquidity floods in. EUR/USD typically sweeps the Asian range - either takes out the Asian high to grab buy-side liquidity, then reverses, or sweeps the Asian low and bounces. This is one of the cleanest setups in forex.

The sweep-and-reverse pattern works because real European traders are now placing orders against the overnight noise. If the Asian session drifted up into a level, London usually sweeps it and dumps. If it drifted down, London sweeps the low and pumps.

London-NY overlap (08:00 - 12:00 NY)

The Super Bowl of forex. New York desks are now active alongside London - this is the only window where both major dollar centers are open simultaneously. Volume peaks, spreads tighten to their absolute lowest, and the day's real directional move usually happens here.

If EUR/USD is going to break a key level today, it'll do it between 08:30 and 11:00 NY. NFP, CPI, FOMC - all released into this window. This is also where retail traders get murdered because the move happens fast and they're chasing it instead of being positioned ahead.

NY afternoon (12:00 - 17:00 NY)

London desks log off around 12:00 NY. Liquidity drops, spreads slowly widen. The morning trend either continues with smaller follow-through, or fades back toward the day's open. Good for taking partials, not great for new entries.

Rule of thumb: if you didn't enter by 11:00 NY, you missed the move. Wait for tomorrow.

Key levels EUR/USD actually respects

Because liquidity is high, EUR/USD respects technical levels with more discipline than less-traded pairs. The levels that matter, in order of importance:

  1. Previous day's high / low (PDH / PDL) - the single most-respected intraday level. EUR/USD sweeps PDH or PDL approximately 65% of trading days. When it sweeps and reverses, that's typically the day's setup.
  2. Previous week's high / low (PWH / PWL) - weekly liquidity. When EUR/USD takes one of these out, expect a major reaction either way.
  3. New Week Opening Gap (NWOG) - the gap between Friday's close and Sunday's open. Often gets filled within the first 3 days of the week.
  4. Daily open - where price opened at 17:00 NY (or 00:00 server time on most charts). Strong magnetic level - if price diverges far above or below, it often returns to test it.
  5. Round numbers (1.0800, 1.0850, 1.0900) - psychological levels with real stop clusters. EUR/USD respects these even when they're not technically significant.

ICT/SMC concepts - how they map onto EUR/USD

If you've gone through the main Trading Plan, this should feel like home. EUR/USD is where ICT and SMC concepts work most reliably because the liquidity supports them.

  • Liquidity sweeps (BSL / SSL) - very clean on EUR/USD. The sweep-and-reverse on London open is essentially a Buy Side Liquidity grab or Sell Side Liquidity grab in textbook form.
  • Market Structure Shift (MSS) - when EUR/USD breaks a swing high/low after a sweep, the MSS confirmation is usually clean and tradeable.
  • Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) - EUR/USD typically respects 4H and 1H FVGs as retracement zones with surgical precision. Smaller TF FVGs (5m, 15m) are noisier.
  • Order Blocks (OB) - especially the last down-close candle before an up move (+OB) or last up-close candle before a down move (-OB). High win rate on EUR/USD because liquidity respects those structural points.
  • Killzones (London, NY AM) - the time windows where 80% of the institutional EUR flow happens. Outside the killzones, you're trading noise.

Two practical rules around news:

  • 30 seconds before release - spread blows out 10-20×. Don't enter.
  • First 15-30 seconds after release - explosive but unpredictable. The initial spike is often the wrong direction. Wait for the dust to settle.
  • 5-15 minutes after release - the "real" direction emerges. This is when the actual move happens.

Bread-and-butter setups on EUR/USD

Setup 1 - London Open Sweep & Reverse

Pre-market: mark the Asian session high and low (20:00-02:00 NY). At London open (02:00-05:00 NY), price sweeps one side of the Asian range, then reverses with an MSS on 5m or 15m. Enter on the FVG retest after MSS.

Setup mechanics

Asian range: 1.0840 - 1.0855. Bias from H4: bearish.

London opens, sweeps the Asian high at 1.0855, hits 1.0858, then forms a 15m bearish MSS down through 1.0845.

Wait for retest of the 15m FVG between 1.0848-1.0851. Short with SL above 1.0860 (above the sweep wick). Target: PDL or NY session low.

Typical RR on this setup: 3-5R.

Setup 2 - NY AM Continuation

If London established a clean direction during 02:00-08:00 NY and price is now in the London-NY overlap, the NY AM session often extends the London move. Wait for the first pullback into a 5m or 15m FVG after 08:30 NY and enter in the London direction.

Setup mechanics

London ran EUR/USD up from 1.0840 to 1.0875. Now it's 09:00 NY.

Price pulls back to 1.0863 - that's inside a 15m bullish FVG from the London leg.

Enter long on the FVG retest, SL below the local swing low at 1.0858. Target: the day's range expansion to 1.0890.

Typical RR: 2.5-4R.

Risk profile - what you can actually expect

Typical SL distance on EUR/USD intraday setups: 10-25 pips. The pair is tight enough that you don't need 50+ pip stops. If your setup needs a 60 pip SL on EUR/USD, your analysis is wrong - this isn't gold.

Typical 1R target: 25-50 pips (1:1.5 to 1:3 setups are the norm). Bigger 1:5+ runs happen on news days or when a major structural level breaks.

Pip value reminder: EUR/USD = $10 per 1 standard lot per pip. So a 20 pip SL on 1 lot = $200 risk. With proper lot sizing from the Lot Sizing lesson, you'd be using 0.10-0.25 lot on a $5,000 account for 1% risk.

When not to trade EUR/USD

  • Friday 12:00 NY onwards - liquidity collapses into the weekend, false moves multiply.
  • Asian session - the range is too tight for any setup to give meaningful RR.
  • 30 minutes before any USD red-folder news - spread widens, slippage destroys your edge.
  • FOMC day before the announcement - the entire morning is chop. Wait until 14:00 NY.
  • Quarter-end / month-end - portfolio rebalancing flows can override technicals entirely.
  • The week between Christmas and New Year - skeleton volume, every level is a fake-out.

Common mistakes on EUR/USD

  1. Trading the Asian session because "the chart is moving" - those 5 pip moves are noise, not signal. You'll burn through commission and spread.
  2. Ignoring DXY - if your EUR/USD long has DXY breaking out the same direction, you're fighting the dollar. Pull up DXY before every entry.
  3. Using GBP/USD volatility expectations - "EUR/USD only moved 25 pips today, this is rigged!" No, that's just EUR/USD on a quiet day. Adjust expectations.
  4. Trading through NFP without a plan - either be flat 5 minutes before release, or have a specific plan for after release. Don't get caught holding into it.
  5. Overleveraging because "EUR/USD doesn't move much" - a slow pair with a 4x position is still a 4x risk. Lot size always.

Last words - master this pair first

If you take one thing from this lesson: spend three months trading EUR/USD only. No GBP/USD distractions, no XAU/USD revenge trades, no exotic pair late at night. Just EUR/USD, just London and NY AM, just one or two setups.

After 3 months you'll know the pair. You'll feel when a sweep is real and when it's noise. You'll know when DXY is screaming a fade and when it's confirming the move. You'll have enough sample size to actually trust your stats.

Then - and only then - you graduate to GBP/USD. The next lesson breaks down "The Cable" - the same playbook but louder, faster, with bigger pips and bigger fakeouts. Different beast, same hunting techniques.
