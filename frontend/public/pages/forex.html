Forex basics

Before your first real trade you need to know exactly what you're buying. A pip is not just a pip - for EUR/USD it's $10 per standard lot, for GBP/JPY about $9, for XAU/USD it's $1 (and everything scales with lot size). Without this foundation you can't do risk management.

  • Pip value - how much 1 pip of movement actually earns or costs you
  • Lot sizing - how much to open so you don't die (the golden formula)
  • Leverage - why 1:500 is not a reason to use it
  • Spread - the hidden fee on every entry, and when it kills you

Major Pairs

The three pairs you'll trade for the rest of your career. EUR/USD is your starter pair - tight spread, clean technicals, the easiest one to learn on. GBP/USD ("Cable") is the volatility king - bigger pip ranges, deeper sweeps, London's home turf. USD/JPY is the global fear gauge - JPY strengthens when markets panic, plus it's the only major with 2-decimal pip mechanics. Together they form a system, not three independent pairs.

  • EUR/USD - the world's most traded pair, where you learn the fundamentals
  • GBP/USD ("Cable") - the volatility king, London's playground
  • USD/JPY - the safe-haven pair + the full correlations system

XAU/USD Gold

Gold is the asset everyone trades and almost nobody sizes correctly. It is not a currency - it is dollars per ounce, driven by real yields, the dollar (DXY) and fear. The whole game comes down to one number: 1 standard lot = $100 per $1 move. Learn that, and the risk math behind it, before you ever click buy.

  • Pip & dollar mechanics - why gold isn't a currency, 1 lot = $100 per $1
  • DXY & real yields - the two engines under the gold price
  • Sessions & the NY open - when gold moves, the sweep, risk-off windows
  • Risk & cheatsheet - the $/move sizing math and the seven rules
